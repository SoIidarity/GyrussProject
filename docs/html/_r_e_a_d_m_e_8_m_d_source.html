9 # Game Features and Development
10 ## *Core Features*
11 * Player ship moves smoothly around perimeter of circular game area, can fire bullets to destroy enemy entities.
12 * Collision detection between all relevant entities.
13 * Explosions indicate collisions on relevant events.
14 * Enemy ships spawn two variants. Either of these variants can appear either at edge of game circle, or from at centre in distance.
15 * Enemies change behaviour, and exhibit different flying patterns.
16 * After leaving screen, enemy respawns.
17 * Player has three lives. Upon losing last life, game ends.
18 * If the player kills 100 enemies, then the game ends, with a different Game Over screen, indicating victory.
19 * Meteoroids randomly fly out from centre, they are indestructible and kill the player upon collision.
20 * Satellite enemy types spawn more rarely, in a group of three. Killing all of them upgrades the player weapon. If the player dies, any surviving satellites disapper. Upon death, the player weapon reverts to the basic lazer gun.
21 * The game speed starts slow, and scales up the longer a player lives. Upon death, the game speeds resets down.
22 * Visual window effects and sounds upon player death event.
23 
24 ### SplashScreen:
25 * Implements splash screen on application load.
26 * User Controls information displayed.
27 * Application icon (multiplatform).
28 * Window can be closed in SplashScreen.
29 * Instructions on how to proceed ( no longer "any key", to prevent accidental start).
30 * SplashScreen is animated (visual cue that game is not frozen).
31 * Quit hot-key (Ctrl-Q) works in SplashScreen and main application
32 * SplashScreen is frame-limited to 60 fps.
33 
34 ### PlayerShip:
35 * PlayerShip rotates around circle on screen in response to player keyboard input, with visual cues of movment (animated sprite) and sound for actions.
36 * Bullets can be fired, originating from the player location towards the centre.
37 * Bullets are correctly oriented to angle of firing.
38 * Bullets are animated.
39 * Player dies if hit by a bullet, meteoroid or enemy ship.
40 * Upon respawn, the player is briefly invulnerable. A visual shield animation indicates this temporary state.
41 
42 ### HUD:
43 * A score is shown during gameplay.
44 * HUD shows player state (alive/invulnerable/dead).
45 * HUD shows how long the player has been alive.
46 * Killing enemies awards points (depending on enemy type).
47 * Score also accumulates according to length of life.
48 
49 ### Entity behavior:
50 * Bullets and Enemies scale when moving around play arena.
51 * Enemies orient to direction of movement.
52 * Behaviours include Hovering, spiralling in, spiralling out, and circling around.
53 
54 ### Object-Oriented design:
55 * ResourceHolder template class for flexible handling of different resource types.
56 * Seperate SoundHolder object controls and plays all sounds and music.
57 * InputHandler only handles keyboard press/release events.
58 * PlayerShip/EnemyShip/Bullet/Meteoroid all derived classed from Base Entity class.
59 
60 ### Other Application features:
61 * Highscore stored and displayed between games.
62 * Player performance metrics shown on Game Over Screen.
63 * StarField effect (immersion and visual complexity.)
64 * Improved Game art assets and sound.
65 * Varied, looping music soundtrack.
66 * Frame limited by explicit time-step (60 frames per second) and uses VSync.
67 * Basic separation of entity/setPlayerShipMove/draw methods.
68 * Early Doxygen documentation (incomplete)
69 * Included CMakeLists.txt for ease of compiling (works multiplatform OSX and Win32.)
70 * Developer mode (in OSX only) has "cheat" hotkeys for adjusting gameplay
